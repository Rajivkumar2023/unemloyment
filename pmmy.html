Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana

PMMY
Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) is a government scheme launched by the Indian government in 2015. It aims to provide financial support to small and micro-enterprises in the country. The primary objective of PMMY is to promote entrepreneurship and generate employment opportunities by offering loans to non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises. The scheme provides funding for various business activities, such as trading, manufacturing, services, and agricultural activities (excluding crop production). Under PMMY, loans are categorized into three categories: 1. Shishu: Loans up to Rs. 50,000 are provided to new enterprises and startups. 2. Kishor: Loans ranging from Rs. 50,001 to Rs. 5,00,000 are provided for established businesses looking to expand. 3. Tarun: Loans ranging from Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000 are provided to enterprises that require higher loan amounts for expansion and growth. These loans are offered by various financial institutions, including banks, microfinance institutions, and non-banking financial companies (NBFCs), under the supervision of the Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency (MUDRA). The interest rates for PMMY loans are determined by the lending institutions based on factors such as the borrower's creditworthiness, the loan amount, and other relevant considerations.
